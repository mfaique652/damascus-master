How to Update Your Domain DNS

  1. Log in to your domain registrar’s dashboard (e.g., GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains).
  2. Find the DNS Management or Zone Editor section.
  3. Edit the A record to point to your server’s public IP address.
  4. For email or subdomains, add CNAME, MX, or TXT records as needed.
  5. Save changes and wait for DNS propagation (up to 24 hours).
If you need help, contact your registrar’s support or ask your hosting provider for your server IP.
After DNS is updated, your site and payment gateway will work with your domain.
